The only thing I think is genuinely risky is the matching logic. A global registry can quickly become noisy if substring matching is too permissive.

My recommendation

I would approve the milestone after asking for six small amendments, not redesign it.

Those amendments are:

Remove the database foreign key wording for connectorId.
Add alias match modes (exact, contains, domain).
Clarify safe hostname matching.
Lock auto-detection after manual connector selection.
Explicitly support platform connectors like Pepper that service multiple distributors.
Define the lifecycle for rejected submissions.

If those are incorporated, I think this becomes a very solid foundation that can scale as FnB Cost Pro grows. It's the kind of architectural investment that will pay dividends when you're adding your 20th or 50th procurement connector rather than your second.
